About Me

Krysta Madewell

I am a hapa Fil-Am designer, born and raised in the SF Bay Area, then moved to every corner of California, and always ended up back in the Bay like a magnet. Digital art and old-school web design were some of my favorite creative hobbies growing up. After exploring different roadmaps like game design, animation, and marketing design under the Media Arts program at Santa Barbara City College, I set off back home (again) to CSU East Bay, where I received a BA in Graphic Design.

Somewhere along the post-grad line, I moved to the DC Metro area, providing visual, interactive, and marketing solutions to the DoD as well as freelance design/art services to keep the variety going. Through my hybrid role, I found a passion for designing interactive and equitable journeys for others and decided to switch my focus to UX design. I then took full advantage of Google's immersive UX Design Certification Bootcamp and dedicated my time to refining my UX-thinking and practices.

In 2021, the magnetic forces of home called upon me again and I ended up relocating back to the Bay Area. I am now currently looking for new ventures in Visual and Interactive-focused Product and UX Design opportunities.
