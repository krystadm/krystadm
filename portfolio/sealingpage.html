SealingTech

Project Summary

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first official in-house designer establishing a cohesive brand identity and guiding traditional marketing design initiatives. Over time, my skills in layout and drive for accessibility proved useful in working with various teams to guide the UX/UI process, which resulted in a redesign of SealingTech's original website.

Building, Securing, and Solving a Digital Lifecycle.

Project Type

Responsive Mobile Site with CRM Functionality, Design System

Duration

April 2020 – June 2020

Role

UX/UI, Visual Design, Interaction Design, Marketing Strategy

Tools

Adobe XD, Illustrator, Photoshop, Pardot, Wordpress

Problem

For an IT solutions provider serving the federal government that also wants to expand its market by manufacturing products, there needs to be a way to bridge the communication between customer and sales and meet federal accessibility guidelines. How do we "modernize" the visual identity of SealingTech, improve accessibility, showcase products, and automate communication for marketing and sales teams?

Solution

A redesigned website including the developing design system, optimized content, and introducing a digital product lifecycle journey with Salesforce/Pardot integration will help solve problems and bring more conversions, brand assimilation, and revenue for the company.

Auditing the Old Website

The old website was chock full of dark patterns and dead ends that have disrupted many of the user journeys for sales, employment opportunities, and customer support. There was no way to get information for or purchase products, applicants could not find our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages, and no way for existing customers to get support. Through a heuristic approach, we walked through the original website with a web developer to pinpoint where exactly we needed to make changes and what we needed to add.

Homepage Audit

Annotation of the homepage based on feedback from users as well as heuristic guidance to understand the problems.

Sitemapping + Early-Stage Iterations

We needed to reorganize the website architecture based on the main goal of reducing the customer journey time and leading to a clear CTA for successful conversions. With my team's onboarding of Salesforce Lightning Cloud with Pardot, internal teams would have the automation for lead capture. Whichever page users signed up from would categorize their information into lists based on the page's main service, and connect them to the right team. It was important to divide pages into specific categories with their own individual goal.

Reworking Navigation

Navigational architecture and site content positioning done on LucidChart.

Mid-Fi Wireframing

Low-fidelity mockups using first reiteration of design system which was a darker theme than the final version.

Stakeholder Reviews + Applying Insights

After coming up with visual design solutions and user flows for the product page (e-commerce functionality) and customer support (live chat) journeys, we tested the ideas with our executive stakeholders within the company and others within the meeting to see what they had to say. While the visual implementations were evolving with support and approval of the team, there were technical and security concerns which kind of laid in irony with the mission of the company.

Complications

Issues with the "E-Commerce" Model:

Because the hardware series has powerful and customizable hardware specs which put a lively price on high-grade technology, there will be complications developing a secure and automated "order form" as well as vetting the contacts. (This was solved with a "Get Quote" function with a form that only accepts business emails and reCaptcha.)

"Live Chat" Support:

Live chat was an idea brought on by marketing to capture leads at the same time as providing support for customers. As a federal contractor, stakeholders had concerns on vetting these contacts securely and manning the support with a small team. (This idea was scrapped and later replaced with a Zendesk portal.)

Hi-Fi Mockups

High-fidelity mockup of approved design. I was directed to visually "brighten up" my initial mockups and implement more red.

Self-Reflecting + Reiterating

Since this was my first major UX project, it definitely wasn't easy, and I was grateful for the support of our third-party development along the way who taught me how to make their jobs easier. I missed a lot of important steps in my process, some were done incorrectly, and I had to prioritize stakeholder direction over user and heuristic insights which might have improved usability. But in hindsight, it was a good start, and I made sure to document and organize what I could to process for ongoing updates in the future, such as:

  • Adding more secondary styling for contrast
  • Limiting embedded video content
  • Guidance on the product widgets

    • The CRM which my team onboarded with, was a great way to gather and organize information internally, as most of our customer information was manually captured on spreadsheets through business card information. As a matter of fact, SealingTech made its first digital sale a few months after the relaunch of the website directly through my team's implementations alone.

    Redesign: Product Page v3

    Technical Changes

    There was a critical issue where customers under federal ISPs could not access our (former) popup forms. We decided to A/B test the embedded form which we developed for the software beta testing.

    Content Changes

    The 360 product module needed more emphasis, so I added an off-white background and an icon for clearer indication. The product pages needed more content on their use-cases, so I added descriptive tabs as a preface to their technical details.

    Styling Changes

    As the marketing's campaign visuals were updated for 2021, I took inspiration from my product slick designs to bring consistency between our print and digital image.

    Bonus: Identity Guide

    I was SealingTech's first in-house designer , which meant many of the style guidelines, assets, and systems were not established. But it was an identity that I needed to tread extremely carefully with, so I took a lot of inspiration from a few freelance projects made before my time, and evolved those ideas gradually to match leadership's vision, Digital marketing, sales, and product/service delivery grew rapidly, especially with the pandemic of Covid-19, which meant shifting to an information format that needed to be easy to understand and also came with the challenge of meeting federal accessibility guidelines.
