Sealing Technologies

Marketing Design, Brand Identity, UI/UX, Research

As one of SealingTech's first hybrid designer/digital marketer, it was my goal to produce a flexible and consistent visual image for the company as we developed a marketing strategy to bring in more leads and sell our new products within the B2B and B2G industry. My responsibility was developing a brand guide and update all marketing, conference, and internal collateral, as well as establish a presence the digital space. In the span of a year, we have expanded on software and hardware products, as well as gained acquisition of Quark Security, Inc.

Website Redesign Overview

SealingTech's top priority was to modernize and establish a proper web presence. Along with the acquisition of Quark and their Linux and Mobile-based security capabilities, we needed a strategy to expand into the hardware/software industry in addition to B2G services. We needed to fix the issues applying to the old website which was roughly built on Wordpress CMS, and address customer and employee concerns regarding the organization and design of the website.

Issues:
  • No trace of brand identity (logos, fonts, colors)
  • Confusing navigation
  • Unoptimized content
  • Unclear journey for customers/applicants
  • No CRM Connection
  • Lack of contrast and hierarchy

    • Research

    We conducted research on large-scale defense contractors and hardware companies with similar B2G and B2B services, like Raytheon and Dell, to get an idea of effective digital marketing and customer journey/retention strategies in the Federal industry. We found that the most effective way to track these journeys and generate leads was through a C2A strategy for product quotes and spec sheet downloads followed by a drip campaign strategy through Salesforce and Pardot connectors.
