Sealing Technologies, Inc.

Visual Design, Marketing Strategy, Web UI/UX

SealingTech is a service provider and contractor based in the DC-Metro area that offers cybersecurity-based engineering services, as well as and hardware and software products within the federal defense sector. In addition to providing design for the marketing team, I worked with stakeholders, engineers, and industry partners by providing technical graphics for proposals, content copywriting for web and social media, and performed user and market research.

Brand and Marketing Design

It was my goal to produce a flexible and consistent visual image for the company as we developed strategies to bring in more leads within the B2G industry. My responsibility was developing a new design system in effort of revamping past and future collateral, as well as designing for web content, landing pages, and email campaigns.

The SealingTech marketing team has been successful in launching a website redesign, implementing marketing automation, and product campaigns, resulting in an increase in leads, content and social media engagement, employment, and providing a better sales and user journey.

Website Redesign

SealingTech's top priority was to modernize and establish a proper web presence to generate leads, automate sales processes, increase engagement, and provide a straightforward journey for applicants. We needed to fix the issues applying to the old website which was roughly built on Wordpress CMS, and address customer and employee concerns regarding the organization and design of the website, and rather than rearrange content, we decided to overhaul the entire website and experience.

    • Research

    We conducted research on large-scale defense contractors and hardware companies with similar B2G and B2B services, like Raytheon and Dell, to get an idea of effective digital marketing and customer journey/retention strategies in the Federal industry. We found that the most effective way to track these journeys and generate leads was through a C2A strategy for product quotes and spec sheet downloads followed by a drip campaign strategy through Salesforce and Pardot connectors.

    Final Thoughts

    I am glad the relaunch was successful despite the timecrunch! Because of our revamped web presence, we were able to increase engagement and bring in conversions. Overall, we made a huge improvement in comparison to the original website and it was well-received by the company and our customers. Personally, this was a journey where I realized that I wanted to grow as a UX designer and experienced the differences and overlap between UX Design and Marketing first-hand.

    Revisiting the project a few months after, there were a few aspects I would consider changing with the live website to help with visual improvements, user accessibility, and mobile responsiveness, specifically for the home and product pages.
