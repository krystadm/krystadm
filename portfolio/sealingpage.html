SealingTech

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first official in-house designer establishing a cohesive brand identity and guiding traditional marketing design initiatives. Over time, my skills in layout and drive for accessibility proved useful in working with various teams to guide the UX/UI process, which resulted in a redesign of SealingTech's original website.

.01 Overview

How might we improve the identity, accessibility, and product journey for the company?

Duration

April 2020 â€“ June 2020

Role

UX Researcher, Visual Design, Interaction Design

Tools

Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop

Problem

For an IT solutions provider serving the federal government that also wants to expand its market by manufacturing products, there needs to be a way to bridge the communication between customer and sales and meet federal accessibility guidelines.

Solution

A redesigned website including the developing design system, optimized content, and introducing a digital product lifecycle journey with Salesforce/Pardot integration will help solve problems and bring more conversions, brand assimilation, and revenue for the company.

.02 Understand

Auditing the Prior User Journey

The old website was chock full of dark patterns and dead ends that have disrupted many of the user journeys for sales, employment opportunities, and customer support. There was no way to get information for or purchase products, applicants could not find our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages, and no way for existing customers to get support. Through a heuristic approach, we walked through the original website with a web developer to pinpoint where exactly we needed to make changes and what we needed to add.

Website Annotation

I compiled an annotation of the homepage based on feedback from users as well as heuristic guidance to understand the problems.

.03 Define

Defining the Journey

We needed to reorganize the website architecture based on the main goal of reducing the customer journey time and leading to a clear CTA for successful conversions. With my team's onboarding of Salesforce Lightning Cloud with Pardot, internal teams would have the automation for lead capture. Whichever page users signed up from would categorize their information into lists based on the page's main service, and connect them to the right team. It was important to divide pages into specific categories with their own individual goal.

.04 Ideate

Ideating the Solution

Visual design was difficult at this stage since the brand's identity wasn't fully developed yet. There was a lot of zigzagging between design themes. As the other deliverables I was creating were becoming better developed, I was able to translate those ideas into the mockups for our website and tailor them over time to be more usable. Since I was going along with marketing's ideas for the website, I decided to mock everything up in mid-fidelity to give stakeholders a better picture and example of what the marketing team was going for.

Mid-fi Mockups

I mocked up the main pages that correlated to the user journey for communicating products and services. Stakeholders weren't sure how they wanted to tailor the hardware product journey, so they wanted to see what it would look like in an "e-commerce" format.

.05 Test

Testing and Gathering Insights

After coming up with visual design solutions and user flows for the product page (e-commerce functionality) and customer support (live chat) journeys, we tested the ideas with our executive stakeholders within the company and others within the meeting to see what they had to say. While the visual implementations were evolving with support and approval of the team, there were technical and security concerns which kind of laid in irony with the mission of the company.

Issues with the "E-Commerce" Model:

Because the hardware series has powerful and customizable hardware specs which put a lively price on high-grade technology, there will be complications developing a secure and automated "order form" as well as vetting the contacts.

Solution

This was solved with a "Get Quote" function with a form that only accepts business emails and reCaptcha.

"Live Chat" Support:

Live chat was an idea brought on by marketing to capture leads at the same time as providing support for customers. As a federal contractor, stakeholders had concerns on vetting these contacts securely and manning the support with a small team.

Solution

This idea was scrapped and later replaced with a Zendesk portal.

.06 Iterate

Iterating the Final Result

Taking stakeholder insights into consideration, I continued on to create high-fidelity mockups for the development team. Because of setbacks out of the teams' control, I was not able to redesign and create every new page as planned, so I created detailed examples that development could freestyle with other pages.

Final Mockups

High-fidelity mockup of approved design. I was directed to visually "brighten up" my initial mockups and implement more red.

Reiterating the Product Page

Technical Changes

There was a critical issue where customers under federal ISPs could not access our (former) popup forms. We decided to A/B test the embedded form which we developed for the software beta testing.

Content Changes

The 360 product module needed more emphasis, so I added an off-white background and an icon for clearer indication. The product pages needed more content on their use-cases, so I added descriptive tabs as a preface to their technical details.

Styling Changes

As the marketing's campaign visuals were updated for 2021, I took inspiration from my product slick designs to bring consistency between our print and digital image.

.07 Reflect

Reflecting on the Process

Since this was my first major UX project, it definitely wasn't easy, and I was grateful for the support of our third-party development along the way who taught me how to make their jobs easier. I missed a lot of important steps in my process, some were done incorrectly, and I had to prioritize stakeholder direction over user and heuristic insights which might have improved usability. But in hindsight, it was a good start, and I made sure to document and organize what I could to process for ongoing updates in the future.

Lessons Learned

  • Advocate a balance between user and stakeholder insights
  • Skipping usability testing can run a budget up
  • Designing without an identity and design system is HARD.

What I Would Do Differently

  • Adding improved solutions to meet accessibility standards
  • Implement more usability testing between changes
  • Have a better-developed design library for consistent visuals

Identity Guide

I was SealingTech's first in-house designer , which meant many of the style guidelines, assets, and systems were not established. But it was an identity that I needed to tread extremely carefully with, so I took a lot of inspiration from a few freelance projects made before my time, and evolved those ideas gradually to match leadership's vision, Digital marketing, sales, and product/service delivery grew rapidly, especially with the pandemic of Covid-19, which meant shifting to an information format that needed to be easy to understand and also came with the challenge of meeting federal accessibility guidelines.
