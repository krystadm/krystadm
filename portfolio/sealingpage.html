SealingTech

Project Summary

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first official in-house designer establishing a cohesive brand identity and guiding traditional marketing design initiatives. Over time, my skills in layout and drive for accessibility proved useful in working with various teams to guide the UX/UI process, which resulted in a redesign of SealingTech's original website.

Building, Securing, and Solving a Digital Lifecycle.

Project Type

Responsive Mobile Site with CRM Functionality, Design System

Duration

April 2020 – June 2020

Role

UX/UI, Visual Design, Interaction Design, Marketing Strategy

Tools

Adobe XD, Illustrator, Photoshop, Pardot, Wordpress

Problem

There are difficulties for potential customers, applicants, and even internal teams to access, display or show information pertaining to the company. For an IT solutions provider serving the federal government that also manufactures products, there needs to be a way to bridge the communication between customer and sales and meet federal accessibility guidelines. How do we "modernize" the visual identity of SealingTech, improve accessibility, showcase products, and automate communication for marketing and sales teams?

Solution

A redesigned website including the developing design system, optimized content, and introducing a digital product lifecycle journey with Salesforce/Pardot integration will help solve problems and bring more conversions, brand assimilation, and revenue for the company..

Auditing the Old Website

The old website was chock full of dark patterns and dead ends that have disrupted many of the user journeys for sales, employment opportunities, and customer support. There was no way to get information for or purchase products, applicants could not find our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages, and no way for existing customers to get support. Through a heuristic approach, we walked through the original website with a web developer to pinpoint where exactly we needed to make changes and what we needed to add.

Homepage Audit

Annotation of the homepage based on feedback from users as well as heuristic guidance to understand the problems.

Design Process

First, we needed to reorganize the website architecture based on a 2-click process with the main goal of having clear CTAs for each page to guide the main user journeys in the simplest way possible. To meet product goals, it was important to divide the hardware and software categories into two main pages, then dedicate individual pages to each product to ensure enough information is displayed.

Reworking Navigation

Navigational architecture and site content positioning done on LucidChart.

Mid-Fi Wireframing

Low-fidelity mockups using first reiteration of design system which was a darker theme than the final version.

Hi-Fi Mockups

High-fidelity mockup of approved design. Stakeholders directed to implement more red.

Self-Reflecting + Reiterating

Since I wanted to divide up the space, I added an off-white background and fullscreen icon for clearer indication of a 360-degree preview module. As the brand guide was updated for 2021, I took inspiration from it's whitespace and layout to bring correlation between our print and digital image. To A/B test the forms, I decided to have it embedded to match what SealingTech would have on the Obscurity landing page. As the Server Node series correlated with the Fly Away Kits, I decided to add a "Use-Case" section with tabs to navigate through different options.

Redesigning the Product Page

Approved product page (left), conceptual revision (right).

Bonus: Identity Guide

I was SealingTech's first in-house designer , which meant many of the style guidelines, assets, and systems were not established. But it was an identity that I needed to tread extremely carefully with, so I took a lot of inspiration from a few freelance projects made before my time, and evolved those ideas gradually to match leadership's vision, Digital marketing, sales, and product/service delivery grew rapidly, especially with the pandemic of Covid-19, which meant shifting to an information format that needed to be easy to understand and also came with the challenge of meeting federal accessibility guidelines
