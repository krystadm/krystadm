SealingTech

Visual Design | Marketing Strategy | Web UI/UX

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first and only official in-house designer, working flexibily with other teams like business development, engineering, third-party partners, and even our customers. My main responsibility was developing a consistent design system over time which would reflect the SealingTech identity to all internal and external materials. I also helped product and engineering teams with visualizing data flows and information architecture content writing, and digital marketing.

Brand Refresh

Since I was the sole designer, I was under a marketing manager who was also a new addition to the company, and together, we came up with digital marketing strategies backed up with the automation, research, and analysis of IT solutions and cybersecurity companies on the B2B and B2C spectrum. In order to modernize, a huge task we needed to tackle was modernizing our identity. While I developed a basic identity guide in my early months at SealingTech, I went on to design marketing collateral based on consistent layouts and visuals, ultimately settling on rules for paragraph styling, presentation formats, iconography, web assets, and message positioning. The led to my contribution of a new overall design system, maintaining our web presence through produced content, and assisting with SEO and web analytics.

As we were introducing these changes, COVID-19 changed many processes drastically for the traditional nature of the Federal industry and instantly relied on things like digital events, content production, and digital communication to keep lead generation going. In my benefit, this sudden switch gave me the opportunity to expand on design.

Website Redesign

What was originally a plan to be an aesthetic update resulted in a functional overhaul which made the website serve as a central communication and information hub for SealingTech.

As we recently introduced Pardot and Salesforce to the company, we were able to conduct a way for the website to automate processes for the sales team, connecting our HR platform to the website, and implementing a Zendesk portal for our customers to get accessible support beyond a phone line.

Original Homepage. Note the lack of branding, content, and confusing navigation.

Updated Homepage layout with a branded color palette featuring services, products, and revised navigation.

Research

Directorates and employeess mentioned that navigating our services and credentials were difficult, and applicants could not easily locate the link to our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages.

We conducted research on local competitors, large-scale defense and hardware companies with similar B2G and B2B services, like Raytheon and Dell, to get an idea of effective navigational architecture, CTA strategies, and customer journey/retention strategies in the Federal industry.

Navigation and content planning done on LucidChart.

Process

Although I was part of the initial planning and design stages, I was reallocated to a another project at the time and mockups were reassigned to a contract designer. My role was reassigned to me in a time crunch 2 months til launch due to web development and brand consistency conflicts with prior mockups. I was able to turn some of the difficulties around and have the website's design reflect common design themes from the design system's development so far, and reuseuse some of the early wireframe material that were originally created during the preliminary stages. It was recommended that I recreate some of contract deisgner's static graphics to aid the communication throughout the website and ensure they were consistent.

After the homepage mockup was completed by me, our Wordpress developers at Webdogs were advised by the marketing manager to use it as reference for other pages, so I only focused on the pages I was assigned to, such as "careers: and "insights".

Wireframes and rough visual ideas.

Early design theme.

What'd I Learn?

For a project kickoff that had many topsy-turvy obstacles and schedule changes, it was definitely one of my favorite things I've done at SealingTech! Putting an early-career designer at the front lines of such a big project at the peak of mandated remote work is sure to be scary, but there is always room to learn and improve.

Revisiting the project a few months after, there were a few aspects I would consider changing with the live website that I learned along the way, including, but not limited to:

  • Visual Improvements
  • WCAG and Section 508 Accessibility
  • Mobile Responsiveness
