SealingTech

UX | Visual Design | Marketing Strategy

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first official in-house designer establishing a cohesive brand identity and guiding design initiatives. Over time, I was able to collaborate crossfunctionally with our executives, sales, engineering, and product teams, and gained the technical sense to articulate design capabilities and limitations to stakeholders, collaborate on automation-driven marketing strategies, as well as guide UX/UI initiatives for web and software platforms. Because of federal requirements, I also shifted SealingTech's digital presence to meet Section 508 accessibility standards.

    Research

    Since I was a part of the marketing team, I was able to work directly with business development on visualizing solution architectures for federal RFPs. Through this I was able to directly identify our target market and customer lifecycle journeys. This was an effective base for identifying competitors and other service providers within our niche industry, so as a marketing team, we researched local competitors, large-scale defense and hardware companies with similar B2G and B2B services, like Raytheon and Dell, to get an idea of how they position themselves through their brand identity, content positioning, website architecture, and campaign strategies.

    Next, we needed to identify hurdles within our digital presence that might disrupt the cycle for our customers. Directorates and employeess mentioned that navigating our services and credentials were difficult, and applicants could not easily locate the link to our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages. Through a heuristic approach, we walked through the original website with a web developer to pinpoint where exactly we needed to make changes and what we needed to add.

    Process

    First, the marketing manager and I organized the navigational architecture based on a 2-click marketing process with the main goal of having clear CTAs for each page. I was able to pull accessible design themes from early collateral to make wireframe material and low fidelity mockups to frame out some of the new content from the marketing team. Mid-development, we had to pause due to our original web developer leaving the project and the previous manager departing from the company.

    Later, we onboarded Webdogs, a web development agency, and restarted the project. By that time, I was able to develop the brand's identity more and update my previous designs.
