SealingTech

Visual Design | Marketing Strategy | Web UI/UX

Sealing Technologies, Inc. (SealingTech) is a federal defense contractor based in the DC-Metro area. I joined the team in 2019 as the first and only official in-house designer, working flexibily with other teams like business development, engineering, third-party partners, and even our customers. My main responsibility was developing a consistent design system over time which would reflect the SealingTech identity to all internal and external materials. I also helped product and engineering teams with visualizing data flows and information architecture content writing, and digital marketing.

Brand Refresh

SealingTech hired freelancers a few times prior to my onboarding to create collateral for past conference seasons, but there were inconsistent design choices that needed to be re-strategized. When I was hired, I did not have much source material besides early drafts that were found in people's inboxes and locked print proofs that I was able to get from our print vendor. Many of the local files I was able to find in old Sharepoint folders had modified text and color swatches, so I wasn't able to figure out which was "correct".

It was requested by the business development team that I recreate the brand guide. The riskiest thing I could do was make extravagant changes in one go, so I decided to change designs in increments so we could reach a proper level of consistency by the 3rd or 4th iterations. The most difficult part was coming up with my own "guidelines" based on what I found floating around and their mutual qualities which I could piece together to create an identity.

The only consistent asset was the logo. There were many versions with questionable modifications, but it stayed relatively the same in terms of shape. I resupplied everyone with one final version which would reflect the official brand colors.

Because the lightning bolt was symbolic to the company's mission, I decided to play with the shape more and use it as an abstract motif which would be common in most collateral. The older product slicks had an excessive variation of paragraph styling, and over time, I was able to limit and maintain a consistent set of paragraph styles across collateral .

Website Redesign

What was originally a plan to be an aesthetic update resulted in a functional overhaul which made the website serve as a central communication and information hub for SealingTech.

As we recently introduced Pardot and Salesforce to the company, we were able to conduct a way for the website to automate processes for the sales team, connecting our HR platform to the website, and implementing a Zendesk portal for our customers to get accessible support beyond a phone line.

Original Homepage. Note the lack of branding, content, and confusing navigation.

Updated Homepage layout with a branded color palette featuring services, products, and revised navigation.

Research

Directorates and employeess mentioned that navigating our services and credentials were difficult, and applicants could not easily locate the link to our Applicant Manager due to the careers section being spread across multiple pages.

We conducted research on local competitors, large-scale defense and hardware companies with similar B2G and B2B services, like Raytheon and Dell, to get an idea of effective navigational architecture, CTA strategies, and customer journey/retention strategies in the Federal industry.

Navigation and content planning done on LucidChart.

Process

First, the marketing manager and I organized the navigational architecture based on a 2-click marketing process with the main goal of having clear CTAs for each page. I was able to pull accessible design themes from early collateral to make wireframe material and low fidelity mockups to frame out some of the new content from the marketing team. Mid-development, we had to pause due to our original web developer leaving the project and the previous manager departing from the company.

Later, we onboarded Webdogs, a web development agency, and restarted the project. By that time, I was able to develop the brand's identity more and update my previous designs.
