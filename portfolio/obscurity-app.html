Quark Obscurity

Identity Refresh | Mobile UX Design | Landing Page Design

Quark Security recently went through a merger with SealingTech, and aimed to have a January 2021 launch for their consumer-level MDM application for Android, Quark Obscurity. This app was in development before the merger, and while it was nearly completed, it needed to work out some flaws and receive a visual rehash. I worked with Obscurity's engineering team to understand the functionality of the app to better understand what design changes were needed using UX best practices. For the landing page, I worked with the marketing manager and our development partners at SaleslabX and WebDogs to learn the best practices in order to create a landing page that had backend functionality for generating leads.

As of now, this project is still in development, but please check for updates!

Research

Quark had already performed their research stages for the app initially with a group of UMD students. The intention was to market towards people who'd need the technology to secure family devices, but the reality was that the app was too complex for the average person because they'd have to technically consider the use of variables and functions with the potential of disabling features on their device. Testing continued during re-development with security engineers to improve its functionality and correct major design flaws. Since it was too late to change the development of the app, it was mutually agreed that we market towards the technically-savvy.

On rebranding, I had a research session with the marketing manager to see who were our most relative competitors and how they positioned themselves on a marketing aspect. I researched design trends, best design practices for security/MDM apps, and up-to-date on most recent changes to Material Design in order to set limitations with Obscurity's developers. Working with Saleslab, I relayed my findings to ensure the design trends and guidelines I were visualizing for Obscurity's rebrand were consistent with current practices, but maintained an identity that was unique to SealingTech.

Logo Redesign merging Quark's original Obscurity logo and SealingTech's palette and minimal design.

UI Redesign Process

Because the app was already developed and had a rushed launch date, there were things I was able to change, but a few aspects are still being worked out. It was the first time I've worked on a software interface, and since the development team had never worked with a designer, I had to study the processes of Product Design on an intricate level and simplify the delivery of visual changes needed that were easy to understand.

I created a style guide for the app based on SealingTech's design system, and specified styles for certain assets, typography, and interaction flows. Recreating the main screens and presenting them was the easiest way for me to deliver a functional vision. Because a prototype did not exist, I offered to create one for the team to avoid the lengthy process in the future of experimenting with the code directly.

Updated login screen concept. Original had no labels and the "eye" changed red to show incorrect password input, which wasn't clear to the user without context. I simplified that idea and created a standard login screen with an error message, and an alternative option in case the user forgot their password.

Website

Because of Google bots and SEO, we decided to keep the Obscurity landing page on the same domain as the SealingTech website. Because Obscurity is starting in alpha stage, we needed a way to get testers to sign up and retain a relationship through marketing automation, but there were a few restrictions.

  • It only ran on Android.
  • Phase 1 required speficially Gmail addresses for invites.
  • The app navigation was too complex for simple feature callouts.
  • The app was not finished with development.

    • The parent SealingTech website had been using pop-up forms since the relaunch, so we ultimately decided to A/B Test the effects on having an "embedded form" version to see how it affected our Analytics. I made two respective versions just in case, and we decided to go with an upgraded version of the "embedded form" page. This came with a subtask of creating user-friendly "sign-up", so I fully prototyped several responsive forms and passed them off to a development team at SaleslabX to create a working iframe which would then be shared with Webdogs.
