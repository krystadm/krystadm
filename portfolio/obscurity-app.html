Quark Obscurity

Identity Refresh | Mobile UX Design | Landing Page Design

Quark had been previously working on a consumer-level MDM application for Android before the merge with SealingTech. In November of 2020, the Quark team picked up on Obscurity's development and predicted a launch date of early 2021. As it was difficult to run a campaign when the app carried a different visual identity, I was put in charge of doing a rebrand for Obscurity which led to more design processes including a responsive landing page and mobile UX design.

As of now, this project is still in development, but please check for updates!

Research

Generally, most current-market MDMs are enterprise-level and cater to organizations that offer employee devices. A unique factor I found was that with the 2020 COVID-19 pandemic, MDMs have a new opportunity to be utilized at a consumer level for parents who might need amped-up security on their children's devices for protection and monitoring usage with entertainment and remote learning. The apps features were also useful for creating variables that would trigger secure actions in case someone might lose their device while traveling.

Quark Obscurity's level of detail in custom functions and monitoring settings were beyond the easy usability of new competitors, but because of its intricacy, it provided features useful for those with a tech-savvy background who need those extra layers in securing personal devices.

Logo Redesign merging Quark's original Obscurity logo and SealingTech's palette and minimal design.

Process

Despite changing the logo and implementing their content into SealingTech's collateral style, the app itself still looked different and there was no design team or prototyping process on the development end. I overlapped my web design knowledge into mobile UX, studied Google's Material Design, and ensured the Obscurity team had sufficient design systems to reference.

Color palette I came up with for the app, logotype font, and a few tips for developers.

Updated login screen concept. Original had no labels and the "eye" changed red to show incorrect password input, which wasn't clear to the user without context. I simplified that idea and created a standard login screen with an error message, and an alternative option in case the user forgot their password.

New design system. The app was originally blue/green and the developers weren't sure of how to distribute the new brand colors beyond the red. I kept in mind functionality and used grey/bright red to represent the status of profiles. I also labeled the other tabs for accessibility and changed the black value for inactive tabs.

Website

Because of Google bots and SEO, we decided to keep the Obscurity landing page on the same domain as the SealingTech website. The challenge was creating an informative and consumer-esque landing page while still keeping to the SealingTech website's formula. Because Obscurity is starting in alpha stage, we needed a way to get testers to sign up and retain a relationship through marketing automation, but there were a few restrictions.

  • It only ran on Android.
  • Phase 1 required speficially Gmail addresses for invites.
  • The app navigation was too complex for a walkthrough video.
  • The app was not finished with development.

    • With the delay, I still had to show the team what their landing page would resemble, so I used my screen mockups as placeholders. I came up with 2 iterations for a landing page that had an embedded form process, and a pop-up form process that would be triggered after inputting the user's email address.
