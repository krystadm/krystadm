Quark Obscurity

UX/UI | Product Strategy | Campaign Design

After the Quark Security merger with SealingTech, the product team pushed for an early 2021 launch of Quark's consumer-level MDM security application for Androidâ„¢, Quark Obscurity. Because of the merger, the product engineering team conducted internal tests to address certain usability issues and also aimed for a UI redesign to be cohesive with SealingTech's brand identity.

    • Research

    To get a general idea of the MDM consumer market and user-base, I researched relative consumer MDM competitors, their use cases, and general design trends for the MDM/Security app market. Based on existing data from before Quark's merger, I learned that Quark Obscurity's initial testing was conducted with a group from UMD's Computer Science department. Comparing this data with the complexity of the app itself showed that this product aimed towards a user that had a technical background and needed purely customizable security settings for their portable devices. MDM competitors had less customizability on a consumer level, and those that did offer customizability operated through an enterprise pricing model not targeted towards individuals.

    UI Redesign Process

    Because the app was already developed and had a rushed launch date, a few solutions I had come up with regarding the main UI were able to be implemented, but the public-facing app generally stayed the same because of development capabilities. Despite demoing a few interactions that were presented and approved by upper management, not much had changed in the public-facing app besides the colors and typography and component states referenced by the design system which was based on Material Design and specified styles for certain assets, typography, and interaction flows.

    Because a prototype for the application did not exist, I decided to create one in Adobe XD to avoid the lengthy process of relaying changes and experimenting with the code directly in the future. As a challenge to myself, I decided that I would just redesign the app and implement the proposed changes in the XD prototype that were approved by stakeholders, but were not present in the prior stage of development.

    Campaign Design

    To amp up interest and public testing, I also collaborated with business development on an interactive direct-to-consumer campaign strategy, which I was in charge of designing CRM-connected campaign deliverables such as an interactive landing page, emails, and survey designs with our marketing agency partners to efficiently gather leads and data for our sales, marketing, and product teams for a successful product launch.

    Landing Page + Emails

    Because of Google bots and SEO, we decided to keep the Obscurity landing page on the same domain as the SealingTech website. The parent SealingTech website had been using pop-up forms since the relaunch, so we ultimately decided to A/B Test the effects on having an "embedded form" version to see how it affected our Analytics.

    I made two respective versions just in case, and we decided to go with an upgraded version of the "embedded form" page. This came with a subtask of creating a user-friendly "sign-up" CTA, so I fully prototyped several responsive forms and passed them off to a development team at SaleslabX to create a working iframe which would then be shared with Webdogs.
