Quark Obscurity

Identity Refresh | Mobile UX Design | Landing Page Design

Quark Security recently went through a merger with SealingTech, and aimed to have a January 2021 launch for their consumer-level MDM application for Android, Quark Obscurity. This app was in development before the merger, and while it was nearly completed with the functional side of development, it needed a visual rehash to be in harmony with SealingTech's brand image. I worked with Obscurity's engineering team to understand the functionality of the app to better understand what design changes were needed using UX best practices. For the landing page, I worked with the marketing manager and our development partners at SaleslabX and WebDogs to learn the best practices in order to create a landing page that had backend functionality for generating leads.

Some features have been redacted to focus solely on the (re)design process, but please check back for more creative updates from my end! :-)

Logo Redesign merging Quark's original Obscurity logo and SealingTech's palette and minimal design.

Research

On rebranding, I had a research session with the marketing manager to see who were our most relative competitors and how they positioned themselves on a marketing aspect. I independently researched design trends, best design practices for security/MDM apps, and took inspiration from SealingTech's developing style guide to create a new identity for Quark Obscurity. I relayed my research and design development over to SalesLabX to strategize on how we could create a landing page design consistent with current practices used for their other customers, but maintained an identity that was unique to SealingTech.

UI Redesign Process

Because the app was already developed and had a rushed launch date, there were things I was able to change, but a few aspects are still being worked out. It was the first time I've worked on a software interface, and since the development team had never worked with a designer, I had to study the processes of Product Design on an intricate level and simplify the delivery of visual changes needed that were easy to understand.

I created a style guide for the app based on SealingTech's design system, and specified styles for certain assets, typography, and interaction flows. Recreating the main screens and presenting them was the easiest way for me to deliver a functional vision. Because a prototype did not exist, I decided to create one in Adobe XD to avoid the lengthy process in the future of relaying changes and experimenting with the code directly. As a challenge to myself, I decided that I would just redesign the app within the boundary of its existing limitations.

Landing Page + Emails

Because of Google bots and SEO, we decided to keep the Obscurity landing page on the same domain as the SealingTech website. Because Obscurity is starting in alpha stage, we needed a way to get testers to sign up and retain a relationship through marketing automation, but there were a few restrictions.

  • It only ran on Android.
  • Phase 1 required speficially Gmail addresses for invites.
  • The app navigation was too complex for simple feature callouts.
  • The app was not finished with development.

    • The parent SealingTech website had been using pop-up forms since the relaunch, so we ultimately decided to A/B Test the effects on having an "embedded form" version to see how it affected our Analytics. I made two respective versions just in case, and we decided to go with an upgraded version of the "embedded form" page. This came with a subtask of creating a user-friendly "sign-up" CTA, so I fully prototyped several responsive forms and passed them off to a development team at SaleslabX to create a working iframe which would then be shared with Webdogs.
