Legal Guardian Gaming

Project Summary

Legal Guardian Gaming is an online community established in 2019 for parents to connect and create content circulating around parenthood and gaming. The client had asked for help on developing the brand, creating a logo, planning a market strategy, and accompanying assets to be used for social media channels.

Being the hero, one win at a time.

Brand Story

Together, the client and I came up with the brand name based on "Legal/Legal Guardian" being the role of a child's caretaker and the Guardian" relating to the hero aspect of typical video game storylines. There were many ideas we played with upon reaching the name, such as names based on "a late night drink", combining thematics of a whiskey glass and baby bottle, or a baby rattle with a controller. Ideally, it needed to correlate on the main idea of "gaming" and "parenthood". eventually leading to a combination of the controller of client's favorite childhood Famicon, shield, and a baby bottle. Combining in "game-like" typography, an accessible color scheme voted on by the community, the brand identity was born.

Preliminaries

Research was done based on the content of popular Twitch streamers and Lifestyle/Family influencers. Because the community was based on gaming and parenthood, it was appropriate to have a parenting blog platform to promote regular content, a Twitter for small updates and taking advantage of trending topics, a Twitch for the main team to be able to host livestreams and talks, and a sub-Reddit with a Discord channel to expand the community and share content.

Logos and brand assets

Process

Branding deliverables and guidelines were finalized with client that included a few different versions for different use-cases, a color palette, and fonts, followed by supporting social media graphics. Something new that was requested was an animated logo, which I had never tried before, but decided to give a shot at my first time using After Effects.

As a bonus, I created an OBS overlay for their Twitch channel which needed to have sections that would identify and differentiate between which team member was streaming and what type game/broadcast they were streaming.
