Bellyrub

Project Overview

Bellyrub is an organic dog food subscription service created for my Google UX Certification capstone project focusing on a web and mobile app where you can sign up to manage your account and create your dog's custom diet profile based on its breed, activity level, general health, and dietary needs.

I'll be updating as I advance through the course. Feel free to check back to follow me on my journey!

Sure to make you howl for joy.

Project Type

Web and Mobile App (iOS), Landing Page Design

Duration

March 2021 – Ongoing

Role

UX Researcher, Visual Design, Interaction Design

Tools

Figma, Adobe XD, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Draw

Problem

More millennials are getting pets alongside their busy schedules, some being rescues who have specific dietary needs or others who just need an optimal formula which cannot easily be found in stores. How can we help these pet owners manage their time, budget, and dog's dietary needs without sacrificing convenience?

Solution

A monthly dog food delivery model that helps owners set up and manage their their subscriptions with easy customizable ordering process for convenience or data-backed generated options to help customers.

Empathizing

In order to create a functional solution for a product, we needed to gather information from a potential user of the target market story to learn their story and identify their goals and frustrations, and align that with the summary of his background. From there, I was able to craft a problem statement which would be helpful in tailoring new features to meet the persona's needs.

A look into the busy life of urban dog owner and software engineer, Tom, who has experienced a career change and relocation which directly affects the reliability, schedule, and convenience of getting his dog's regular food.

User Flows and Storyboarding

Using Tom's story and problem statement, I was able to create a userflow for the app to have a basic structure on interacting and navigating through the screens. Afterwards, I created a big picture and close-up storyboard to capture the scenarios that Tom would most likely be faced with based on his current problems with buying his dog food.

Wireframing and Lo-fi Prototypes

Remember that userflow? Well, it came in handy when I finally had the chance to build the wireframes for the Bellyrub app's interface in combination with the vision I had when I was creating the storyboards, so I decided to expand on it. Using best practices I've gathered from the competitive analysis as well as having user accessibility in mind, I created a clear journey with interactive elements to guide the customer through multiple processes such as account creation, profile creation, 2 different ordering processes based on user preference, and order tracking. I was also able to build a mobile rendition of the homepage and 2 different menus.

Wireframes for the mobile version of the Bellyrub mobile app that feature the homepage, sign up process, meal plan creation, pet profiles, and account settings.
